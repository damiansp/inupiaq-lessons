Introduction

Iñupiaq is the language of the Iñupiaq people, who have traditionally lived in Northern and Northwestern Alaska, and most of whom still do today. The language is closely related to other Inuit languages spoken across the northern border of North America and Greenland, and is part of the larger Eskimo-Aleut language family.

The flashcards presented here may be used independently by anyone interested in learning the language, but are intended to accompany the textbook North Slope Iñupiaq Grammar: First Year, by Edna Ahgeak MacLean, available through The University of Alaska Fairbanks' Alaska Native Language Archive, or through commercial booksellers, such as Amazon.

While the different Iñupiaq dialects are similar, and mostly mutually intelligible, there are some differences in pronunciation, and occasionally in word usage. These materials are based on the North Slope dialect as spoken in Barrow, Alaska, though, if there is interest, we may eventually include other dialects.

We hope this tool will help interested learners. Please feel free to contact us with questions, suggestions, or any other feedback.
